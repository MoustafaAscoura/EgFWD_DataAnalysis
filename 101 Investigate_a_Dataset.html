Dataset Description¶

The following data set contains information about 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings and revenue. It contains different attributes of the movies, these attributes are discussed and descirbed in the following section.

The Dataset Columns:

  1. id
    This is the identification number given to the movie by the TMDb. We will keep using it as our index.
  2. imdb_id
    Id in the IMDB website. We won't use it and it'll be removed later in the cleaning phase.
  3. popularity
    A popularity score for a movie
  4. budget
    The cost of making the movie (USD)
  5. revenue
    Total Revenue of the movie (USD)
  6. original_title
  7. cast
  8. homepage
    Homepage link of the move. We won't use it and it'll be removed later in the cleaning phase.
  9. director
  10. tagline
    Tagline of the movie. We won't use it and it'll be removed later in the cleaning phase.
  11. keywords
    We won't use it and it'll be removed later in the cleaning phase.
  12. overview
    Description of the movie's plot. We won't use it and it'll be removed later in the cleaning phase.
  13. runtime
    Length of the movie (minutes) We won't use it and it'll be removed later in the cleaning phase.
  14. genres
  15. production_companies
  16. release_date
  17. vote_count
    Number of users rated the movie
  18. vote_average
    Average rating of users
  19. release_year
    We won't use it and it'll be removed later in the cleaning phase. As release date is enough and more informative.
  20. budget_adj
    The cost of making a movie in 2010 dollars (Accounting for inflation). We won't use it and it'll be removed later in the cleaning phase.
  21. revenue_adj
    Total Revenue of the movie in 2010 dollars (Accounting for inflation). We won't use it and it'll be removed later in the cleaning phase.

Question(s) for Analysis¶

We impose some questions and we try to asnwer them using the data in our hands. We can judge if a movie is successful or not by a simple index calculated from the following formula:

Profit_margin = (Revenue/Budget - 1)
Success_index = profit_margin/avg.profit_margin + Rating/Avg.rating + popularity/avg.popularity

This will give us an indication of if the the movie is both financially successful and liked by people. And this will help us answer the following questions:

1 - What companies are production successful movies in some genres (Action, Crime, SciFi, and Thriller)?
2 - Is there any relation between the budget of a movie and its success? Are expensive movies more successful?
3 - Are revenues related to the cast? Who is the most success-attracting star?

We will start digging into those questions, given data on the cast, budget, and revenues of the movies.

def plt(gnre,knd,ttle):

    return top

Conclusions¶

We showed which companies are more successful in each genre and how much of the market they take up. This doesn't neccessarly mean that every movie produced in the top companies in the corrosponding genre will be a success.

The second thing we witnessed is that spending more money on a movie slightly affects its success. Success index and budget are not caustively correlated, but they have a weak positive relationship. However, it could the case that more budget results in more revenue, they are more correlated. This could be because that the marketing budget is higher and the movie is more spread around the world.

Last, we listed the top 20 actors who have acted in the highest movies with average success index. Again, we defined the success index as a combination between profit, user rating, and popularity. Some movies are financially successful but not popular and vice versa.
